Microcosm

2D rogue-like game built with Pygame and OpenGL, where you play as a microbe surviving in a hostile petri dish against escalating enemy waves. I implemented procedural encounter generation, adaptive enemy AI with behavior states, and enemy scaling tied to player progression, run duration, and performance. Combat uses frame-precise hit detection, invulnerability windows, and telegraphed attacks, while rendering includes layered particle effects and dynamic lighting passes. I also applied high-level math and competitive-programming style optimization to keep updates efficient under heavy load, including vectorized movement calculations, aggressive state pruning, and low-overhead collision filtering. The result is a fast, replayable loop that stays technically tight and readable even in high-intensity fights.

ACSL Academy

Full-stack ACSL learning platform built with Next.js, Tailwind, and Supabase, then deployed on Vercel for fast global access. It contains 30+ structured lessons across core programming and computer science topics, plus guided quizzes, account-based progress tracking, dedicated practice problem banks, and timed contest-style sets. Students can train by topic, simulate competitive conditions, and review performance history over time. I built the content flow around progressive difficulty so users move from fundamentals into harder logic and implementation patterns without getting stuck early. The platform also supports fast lesson iteration, structured feedback loops, and data-backed refinement of practice sets based on where students struggle most. The goal is to make ACSL preparation consistent, measurable, and genuinely effective for both self-study and coached workflows.

The Legend of Rick

2D platformer built with Godot and GDScript, centered on responsive movement, readable level design, and progression that increases challenge without spiking frustration. The game includes multiple stage archetypes, checkpoint flow, enemy timing systems, and tuned physics for jump arcs, momentum, and recovery windows. I iterated heavily on animation state handling and collision behavior so movement feels smooth and predictable. Beyond core movement, I focused on level pacing, fail-state clarity, and recovery timing so difficult sections feel learnable rather than random. Ongoing work is focused on adding levels, boss-style encounters, and stronger pacing between platforming, combat, and humor beats, while keeping the control feel consistent across all stage types.
